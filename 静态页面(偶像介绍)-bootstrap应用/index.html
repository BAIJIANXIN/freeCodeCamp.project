Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1947 - Born in ShangHai.
  • 1965 - Graduated from Taipei Municipal Jianguo High School.
  • 1969 - Graduated from Electrical Engineering in National Chiao Tung University.
  • 1972 - While working in Seattle, Yang came across the Werner Herzog film Aguirre, the Wrath of God.
  • 1974 - graduate program at the University of Florida, where he received his Masters Degree in Electrical Engineering.
  • 1981 - returned to Taiwan to write the script for and serve as a production aide on a Hong Kong TV Movie,The Winter of 1905.
  • 1983 - While his contemporary Hou Hsiao-hsien focused more on the countryside, Yang was a poet of the city, analyzing the environment and relationships of urban Taiwan in nearly all his films. Yang created first feature film, That Day, on the Beach.
  • 1985 - Yang followed with his second feature film, Taipei Story, where he cast fellow auteur Hou Hsiao-hsien as the lead, a former Little-League baseball star named Lung trying to find his way in Taipei.He married Taiwanese pop-singer and music legend Tsai Chin in May.
  • 1986 - Yang's third feature film was Terrorizers, a complex multi-narrative urban thriller that reflected on city life and contained the crime elements and alienation themes of an Antonioni film. The film also starred Cora Miao and won a Silver Leopard at The Locarno International Film Festival and was examined by Fredric Jameson in The Geopolitical Aesthetic.
  • 1991 - Yang's fourth film was A Brighter Summer Day (The Chinese title of "Gǔ lǐng jiē shàonián shārén shìjiàn" literally translating to: "The Murder Incident of the Boy on Guling Street").The film also won the Best Film award at the 1991 Asia-Pacific Film Festival, a Best Foreign Language Film Director Award for Yang at the 1983 Kinema Jumpo Awards, another Best Director award for Yang at the Nantes Three Continents Festival (where Yang was also nominated for the Golden Montgolfiere award) and the Silver Screen award for "Best Director - Asian Feature Film" for Yang at the Singapore International Film Festival.
  • 1994 - Yang's fifth film was A Confucian Confusion, a multi-character comedy set in urban Taiwan), which was nominated for a Palme d'Or and in competition at the 1994 Cannes Film Festival and also garnered a Golden Horse Award for Best Screenplay Originally Written for The Screen.
  • 1995 - He divorced in August, and he subsequently married pianist Kai-Li Peng (彭鎧立).
  • 1996 - ang's sixth film was Mahjong, a sharp, incisive reflection of modern urban-Taiwan seen through foreign eyes, which also starred several foreign actors, which won an Honourable Mention or The Alfred Bauer Award at the 46th Berlin International Film Festival,The film is also currently available on The Criterion Collection as a "Director-approved Special Edition", and features a newly restored digital transfer a long with a DTS-HD Master Audio soundtrack (on the Blu-ray), audio commentary from Yang and Asian film critic Tony Rayns,a taped interview with film critics Rayns about Yang and the New Taiwanese Cinema movement, the U.S. theatrical trailer, an original English subtitle translation by Yang and Rayns, and an essay by writer Kent Jones as well as notes from Yang himself.
  • 2000 - Yang was best known for his seventh and final film, Yi Yi (full title in some areas: Yi Yi: A One and A Two) - it was for this film that he received the Best Director at the 2000 Cannes Film Festival.
  • 2007 - died at 45 age on June 29 at his home in Beverly Hills, as a result of complications from a seven-year struggle with colon cancer.
